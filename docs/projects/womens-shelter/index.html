Designing for Change: Uppsala Women’s Shelter

An Activist UX Project Focused on Safety, Awareness & Digital Inclusion

Overview

This project was part of a university-level Human-Computer Interaction course with a focus on design activism. Our goal: to create an inclusive, safe, and discreet digital intervention to support women experiencing intimate partner violence—particularly targeting digital and emotional abuse.

In collaboration with Uppsala Women’s Shelter, we focused on how digital design can play an ethical and empowering role.

Design Challenge

  • Create a subtle, safe user experience that protects the user’s identity and behavior.
  • Design for Gen Z and foreign-background women – two underrepresented but vulnerable demographics.
  • Present information in discreet, digestible formats while avoiding potential risks.

Approach & Research

We conducted a series of workshops, competitor analysis (e.g. Safe Horizon, RAINN), and synthesized feedback from survivors and experts. Key takeaways included the importance of subtlety, language accessibility, and mobile-first thinking.

Solution Overview

1. Instagram Ads

Two ad types were created: a Valentine’s Day-themed attention grabber, and an informative story series. Both were visually appealing but subtle, designed to avoid raising suspicion.

2. Enhanced Leave Page Button

We redesigned the “Leave Page” button on the Uppsala Women's Shelter site, increasing its visibility and making it context-aware. We also proposed a browser history disclaimer on first page load.

3. Hidden Support Chat

A business-mimicking chat disguised as a shopping assistant, allowing for discreet communication. Includes auto-reset and a panic “exit chat” shortcut.

Ethical Considerations

Every design decision was filtered through an ethical lens. How might a user be endangered? What happens if their abuser sees the screen? Our aim was to hide support in plain sight.

  • Muted color palettes to avoid visual alarms.
  • Language minimized to avoid flagging attention.
  • Secondary messaging only visible on further interaction.

Tools & Process

Tools: Figma · Canva · HTML/CSS · Google Forms · Affinity Mapping

Process: Research → Ideation → Prototyping → Feedback → Iteration → Final Delivery

Reflection

This was one of the most meaningful projects I’ve worked on. It challenged me not only as a designer, but also as a human. It taught me the importance of designing for real people, in real danger, and how technology must support—not expose—them.
